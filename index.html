Why is it important to purchase insurance?

 

A recurring question may be why have insurance?. Well, insurance or protection plans are relevant if we want to properly manage the finances of people and companies.

 

The importance of insurance

Insurance is important because it is a tool that protects us from possible unforeseen events that will later result in loss of property or income.

Thus, the value that is paid for them, called the insurance premium, generates the peace of mind of having support and, in this sense, more than an expense, insurance is an investment.

This is how events such as disabilities, job loss, natural disasters, accidents, theft, among others, can be insured to protect companies, individuals and their families from the economic consequences.

How to acquire insurance?

Through a contract with the insurer, people or companies agree to pay a premium, in exchange for which they receive protection in certain situations, in order to reduce or eliminate the unwanted financial impact.

Is it necessary to have insurance for each of the unforeseen events?

No, when evaluating the relevance of insurance, the relevance of the risk contextualized with the needs of each individual and the family must be taken into account, in addition to the availability of resources to acquire it.

For example, protecting health is relevant because when a calamity occurs, the associated costs are very high. As for life insurance , it is important if it is the person who generates the family income.

What benefits do you get when buying insurance?

The benefits are not only concentrated in the economic sphere, receiving an amount of money for the unexpected event, but also peace of mind is generated in the family nucleus, an intangible that is difficult to value.

Many insurances, for their part, also have assistance and other added values ​​that can be valuable; the important thing is to know them and make use of them.

 

What is important to consider when buying insurance?

1. Coverage

The insured amount and the events in which you are entitled. Here it is also important to consider the dates between which the risk is insured.

2. Price

The relative value between the premium and the disbursement at the time the insured event occurs. The ability to pay must be evaluated and the value of the periodic or single premium must be adjusted to the cash flow of the person or company.

The price depends on the insured value, generally the higher, the higher the premium. For this reason, when structuring the insurance, the needs must be considered and the risk to be insured must be assessed.

3. Insurance company

The insurance company is the issuer of the policy and as such is the one that has to respond when the event occurs. For this reason, it is important to evaluate the entity and its support, as well as to consider the infrastructure, the service and the speed of response. The trajectory of the insurance company and its experience are also points to take into account.

4. Counterparty

They are the insurance intermediaries and are in charge of distributing the product and giving advice. There are different types of intermediaries, including Family Compensation Funds.

5. Exclusions

Events in which, despite the occurrence of the insured situation, the company is not obliged to pay. To be clear about what you are buying, it is important to find out this point.
